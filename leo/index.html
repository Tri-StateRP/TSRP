Project California RP

🚔 LEO

Policing must be realistic, consistent, and story-driven. The goal is quality RP — not ego, not "winning."

Community Expectations (Give PD RP)
  • Do not intentionally deny PD RP just because you see them around.
  • Seeing illegal activity doesn't mean "insta-chase." Allow scenes to breathe and build naturally.
  • Police baiting is not allowed: no provoking officers for attention, chases, clips, or chaos.
  • If you start something illegal, be prepared for realistic consequences if caught.
Officer Expectations (Realism First)
  • Policing should feel grounded: reasonable suspicion, probable cause, communication, and teamwork.
  • Prioritize de-escalation and scene control when possible.
  • Don't escalate a situation just because you can — match the threat and keep it believable.
  • Professionalism matters: treat people like people, even when they're doing dumb stuff.
Use of Force & Escalation (Chain of Command)
  • Escalation should follow a clear chain of command and communication.
  • When available, supervisors lead major decisions (containment, major tactics, high-risk stops).
  • Force should match the threat level and be used only when justified.
  • Document serious uses of force and be ready to justify actions in-character.
Pursuits & PIT Policy
  • PIT is allowed — but it must be performed safely and away from the general public when possible.
  • Avoid PIT in crowded pedestrian areas, heavy traffic, or high-risk locations unless absolutely necessary.
  • Pursuits should be coordinated: clear comms, units assigned roles, and avoid reckless pile-ups.
  • Risk vs. reward matters: if it's not worth endangering the city, de-escalate.
No Corrupt Cop RP
  • Corrupt cop RP is not allowed in Project California RP.
  • Any officer caught engaging in corruption will face serious consequences.
  • In-character, officers will be seen before a judge if caught — this is treated seriously.
Discipline & Accountability
  • Complaints should be handled through proper channels (reports/tickets), not public arguments.
  • Leadership and staff may review bodycam/scene context to ensure fairness.
  • Patterns of reckless policing, power-tripping, or bad faith behavior will be addressed.
